The Debt Divers Challenge

“The price of liberty is vigilance... and a balanced ledger.” As the galaxy teeters on the brink of chaos, and the cost of war climbs higher than a Helldiver drop pod, Super Earth High Command proudly unveils Debt Divers: a courageous initiative designed to transform battlefield valor into fiscal responsibility. In this bold new directive, every Helldiver is not only a warrior—but a debtor in pursuit of redemption.

Through fire and steel, through bug and bot, your mission is clear: earn Super Credits and chip away at the sacred obligation you owe to Super Earth. In Debt Divers, the enemy isn’t just the Terminid horde... it’s compound interest.

“Remember, Helldiver: every credit you earn is a step closer to freedom—and a step further from financial disgrace. Serve with honor, spend with wisdom, and always pay it forward... with interest.”

Your goal is to have 1000 Super Credits in your account, to pay your way out of debt.
Complete missions to earn Super Credits, which you can spend on equipment in the shop. But be warned: purchasing items in the shop sets you back from accomplishing your goal of accumulating Super Credits!

In order to start a mission, you must equip items in your loadout menu (just like in-game). You must choose 4 stratagems, armor, a primary, a secondary, and a throwable. Click on an item in your Purchased Items box or Default Items box to equip the item. To unequip an item, simply click on it again.

Your Default Items have unlimited uses, but your Purchased Items have uses corresponding to how many times you have purchased it (which you can see in the top right corner of the item).

After each mission, press the 'Mission Complete' button and complete the form. Hover over the text of each input to see how many credits you will earn or lose.

Press the 'Shop' button to view purchaseable items. Click on an item to purchase it. Each time you purchase an item the price will increase for the next time you purchase that same item, with the exception of Super Earth Surplus (a random item).
Prices that are on sale will have a green price tag, and will not increase in price as much as items at full price.

For example: If I purchased the Quasar Cannon at full price for 25 Credits, the price for the next Quasar Cannon will be 30 Credits.

Additionally, the shop will completely reset after each operation is completed.

If you fail a mission, you will not lose credits, but item usage will still be counted. Additionally, your ending score will be negatively affected.

Your progress is saved in your browser's local storage. If you clear your browser's local storage, your progress will be lost. If you want to start a new run, you may do so by pressing the yellow "Restart" button on the right side of the page. Your run will be saved and accessible in the save menu.

Saved games can be loaded, downloaded, and uploaded in the Save Data Management menu, accessible by clicking the floppy disk button in the upper right corner. You can also rename your save files to keep them in order.

  • Q: Who was this challenge made for?

    A: This challenge was made for those who enjoy challenge runs and rogue-like elements, and is friendly to newer players and veterans alike due to the multiple difficulty modes and ability to filter out items from warbonds you might not have purchased.

  • Q: How many squadmates do I need to run this challenge?

    A: This challenge is viable for a squad of participating players and solo players. For solo players, "Easy" mode is recommended.
    Participating squadmates don't need to be on the same mission in order to run the challenge together.

    i.e. You can be several missions ahead of your squadmate, and it doesn't necessarily affect your challenge run.

  • Q: What if I reach 1000 Super Credits before my squadmates?

    A: You can still run the challenge with your squadmates even after you've reached your goal. Simply continue playing as normal. 1000 Super Credits will be deducted from your balance.

  • Q: What if I don't have all the warbonds?

    A: Check out the Settings menu by pressing the blue gear button. From there you can change your difficulty and toggle on/off the warbonds.

  • Q: What is Super Earth Surplus again?

    A: A random item. Specifically, it is a 1 in 6 chance of receiving a random primary, secondary, throwable, stratagem, booster, or armor passive.

  • Q: Why is X item so cheap/expensive?

    A: Every item has a base cost, plus a random number cost added to it (the range of the random number depends on difficulty). The random number usually makes the item more expensive, but there is a chance the cost can be cheaper than the base cost.
